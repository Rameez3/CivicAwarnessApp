The Problem:

Austin, Texas has a major problem: low voting turnout. The voter turnout in Austin is a lot lower than it should be, and this has to change as soon as possible.

The Facts:

Texas by itself is the state with the 5th lowest voter turnout in the entire USA. This affects the voting statistics in Austin as well.

Over the Last 30 years:

  • The highest voter turnout percentage in Austin was 73.68% in 1992
  • The lowest voter turnout percentage was 38.69% in 2014
  • The highest registered voters were in 2018, with 19,233 people.
  • The lowest registered voters were in 1990 with 9,949.
  • The highest voter turnout was in 2016, with 12,273 people.
  • The lowest voter turnout was in 1990, with 5,696 people.

THE STATS

This graph shows the negative trend of voter turnout from 1990-2018. As you can see from the graph, the highest voter turnout was in the 1990's, and it just kept on getting lower and lower. This shows the big problem Austin has with voting, because as the population keeps on rising, the voter turnout percentage decreases year after year.

Even though the graph shows a final increase of 0.75% from 1990 to 2018, this is very misleading for many reasons. One of these reasons is that even thought the voter turnout percentage rose by 0.75%, the population of Austin rose from less than 500,000 to more than 2,000,000 people. This means that there has been very less change from the 1990's to the time that we live in as of now.

As citizens of the United States of America, it is our given responsibility to vote for our leaders and politicians. It was what this country was built upon: the idea of representation. These statistics show the harsh realities of our political time: we aren't voting. This has to change. Especially in a city such as Austin, Texas, which is the capital of the second largest state in the US. Our indifference towards voting can and will affect the future generations of our city, as the politicians in office make changes that will affect the day to day lives of us now and in the years to come. Voting is about the changes that YOU want to see happen to our society.
